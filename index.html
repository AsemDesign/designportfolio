I'm Assem Kiyalova, a designer who got into graphic design to make things look good, and stayed because design can actually do things. Shift power. Complicate narratives. Make the invisible hard to ignore.

I'm finishing my MFA in Design at Rutgers, working at the intersection of care, gender equity, and emerging technology. My projects have tackled domestic violence, systemic inequality, and the way visual systems quietly construct who gets to matter. Spoiler: branding is never just branding.

I'm drawn to the research-heavy, culturally messy questions — the ones where you have to invent the framework before you can design anything. I treat every project like a provocation: what assumption are we stress-testing today?
