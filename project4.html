Don't be a man

An ongoing campaign developed for the HeForShe division of UN Women, Don’t Be a Man challenges entrenched ideas of masculinity across Central Asia.

I led design and art direction within a large, cross-functional team, shaping a campaign aimed at engaging men in conversations around gender equality—an audience often resistant, yet critical, within patriarchal contexts. The strategy reframed masculinity not as something to defend, but to redefine.

The campaign has reached over 20 million people. Its ecosystem spans four short films, a comic series, and a podcast—each designed to meet audiences where they are, speaking in a language that invites dialogue rather than opposition. Grounded in research, including 30 in-depth interviews, the work balances global frameworks with local specificity.

I developed a flexible, multilingual design system implemented across five countries, ensuring visual and narrative coherence while adapting to distinct cultural and linguistic contexts. The result is a campaign that operates at scale without losing nuance—measured not only in reach, but in engagement, conversation, and cultural resonance.
