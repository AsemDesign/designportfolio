Bodies of Flora

What happens when a species disappears twice — once biologically, and again from human memory? This project, led by Professor Sue Huang as Rutgers–New Brunswick Laureate, resurrects extinct New Jersey plants through AI, archival research, and speculative design.

As research assistant and MFA collaborator, I worked directly with preserved specimens from the Chrysler Herbarium and contributed across multiple layers of the project. I designed a custom typeface from the original handwriting of botanist William H. Witte, extracting letterforms from his historic scripts and building a font now used across all project materials. The project website, currently in progress, is inspired by David Hockney collages. From custom software developed within the project, AI-generated 3D models of extinct plants were reconstructed from manuscripts and archival fragments. Those models became the source material for cyanotypes — letting the plants themselves become image, letting them tell their own story.

The project culminates in an exhibition at Mana Contemporary, May 2026.
